Interaction Design North is Canada's premier interaction design master class for designers and developers, hosted by Normative Design.

Join us on July 8 - 10 for an intensive practitioner-led workshop covering the theory and practice of the full interaction design process.

Sign up now ›

Who is it for?

The program is aimed at engineers, designers, and other people working in software, the web, or technology, looking for a deeper understanding of interaction design theory and practice.

What will I learn?

During this three day workshop, you will be guided through the full design process, from discovery and requirements, through concepts and synthesis, to documentation and implementation. You will learn tangible skills that you can use immediately, as well as the theoretical grounding behind the methods.

How much does it cost?

Where is it held?

The workshop will be held at the Normative Design studio - 376 Bathurst St. (just north of Dundas) in Toronto.

Will there be food?

Yes. Lunch, coffee and snacks will be provided on all three days.

Agenda

Day 1: Morning | What is Interaction Design?
Lecture: Introduction to Interaction Design by Matthew Milan
A brief history of Interaction Design and design theory, examining why it is an integral part of the success of any digital product.
Incorporating Interaction Design into your process
How can you use these techniques in your day to day work? Learn where design fits and how you can get the most out of the methods you will learn.
Qualitative Research Methods
Learn qualitative research methods that help you understand your customers from a design perspective.
Day 1: Afternoon | Understanding What to Design
Analyzing Qualitative Research
Research data needs to be analyzed to understand what it all means. You will learn some specific methods of qualitative data analysis that help define the requirements for the design.
Data Modelling
Learn a number of data modelling techniques to structure your analyzed data for use throughout the design process.
Design Requirements and Constraints
Using the analyzed research you will learn how to create design requirements and set constraints on what can an cannot be part of the final design.
Day 2: Morning | From Data to Design
Lecture: Design Practice by Matt Nish-Lapidus
An introduction to the practice of design and the studio environment. This theory will be put into practice over the course of the day.
What is Synthesis?
Synthesis is a key part of the design process, where all the inputs (data, requirements, and constraints) are taken and combined with the designers experience, intuition, and judgement to create new design concepts.
Synthesis Methods
You will practice a number of synthesis methods including affinity diagrams, flow diagrams, concept maps, and more.
Day 2: Afternoon | From Data to Design Part 2
Constructive Sketching
That afternoon session will focus on constructive sketching, our favourite synthesis method.
Selecting, Refining, and Exploring Concepts
Once you’ve generated some ideas, you will start exploring the best ones in more detail, and eliminating ideas that won’t work. In this session you will learn some activities to help decide which concepts to keep and how to dig deeper.
Day 3: Morning | Refining and Solidifying the Design Concept
Lecture: Collaborating Towards Implementation
Taylan Pince and Cameron Westland will talk about how to bridge the gap between design concepts and technology in the development of modern web and mobile apps.
Prototyping Design Ideas
Prototyping is an integral part of the design process. It allows the designer to take their sketches, flows, and other documentation, and see how it works in a more realistic context. In this session you will learn a number of prototyping methods including click-through wireframes, HTML prototypes, and paper prototypes.
Day 3: Afternoon | Testing and Communicating your Design
Testing a Prototype (Design Validation)
Validating the design can take many forms, from formal usability studies to quick questions and demonstrations to colleagues. You will learn about the value of different types of testing and validation, and how they work with the different types of prototypes.
How to Communicate your Design
When the design is ready to build it will need to be communicated to the people involved in the next stages of the project. In this session, you will learn about a number of communication methods, from documentation to demonstration and collaboration.
Iterating Design Through Development and Collaboration
A designer’s job is not done when the project goes into production. You will earn how effective teams collaborate through the entire development process, and how the design can continue to improve through these iterations.
