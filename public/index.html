Interaction Design North is Canada's premier interaction design master class for designers and developers, hosted by Normative Design.

Join us on July 8 - 10 for an intensive practitioner-led workshop covering the theory and practice of the full interaction design process.

Who is it for?

The program is aimed at engineers, designers, and other people working in software, the web, or technology, looking for a deeper understanding of interaction design theory and practice.

What will I learn?

During this three day workshop, you will be guided through the full design process, beginning from discovery and requirements, through concepts and synthesis, and ending with implementation. You will learn tangible skills that they can use immediately, as well as the theoretical grounding to understand why we design in this way.

Who is leading the workshop?

Senior designers from Normative Design, as well as some special guest practitioners. Read more about the workshop facilitators below.

How much does it cost?

Register now for earlybird pricing of $895.

Where is it held?

The workshop will be held at the Normative Design studio - 376 Bathurst St. (just north of Dundas) in Toronto.

Will there be food?

Yes. Lunch, coffee and snacks will be provided on all three days.

Workshop Facilitators

Matt Nish-Lapidus
SR Designer, Normative
@emenel

Matt Nish-Lapidus is a senior designer at Normative Design in Toronto. Matt’s background in new media art, music, and design has enabled him to practice interaction design and related disciplines for over 10 years across a number of industries. He has worked for large clients such as Coca-Cola, as well as helped start-ups design and launch new products. Matt is currently serving on the board of directors for the Interaction Design Association (IxDA) and was a founder of IxDA Toronto.

Matthew Milan
CEO, Normative
@mmilan

Matthew Milan is an interaction designer with a background in software development, spatial technology and urban planning. Over the last ten years, Matthew has led large design and strategy teams, slept under his desk at successful start-ups and tried most of the things he’s been told he couldn’t or shouldn’t. A founding partner at Normative Design in Toronto, Matthew is passionate about the intersection of design methods, network technologies and meatspace.
